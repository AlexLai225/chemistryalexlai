HKDSE Tips

  • Effect of adding H₂SO₄ (acid): Increasing [H⁺(aq)] makes Qc < Kc. To restore equilibrium, the system consumes H⁺ and CrO₄²⁻, producing more Cr₂O₇²⁻. The equilibrium position shifts to the right → solution turns orange.
  • Effect of adding NaOH (alkali): OH⁻ neutralises H⁺, so [H⁺(aq)] decreases and Qc > Kc. The system produces more H⁺ and CrO₄²⁻ by decomposing Cr₂O₇²⁻. The equilibrium position shifts to the left → solution turns yellow.
  • Reaction quotient Qc vs equilibrium constant Kc:
    • Qc < Kc → net forward reaction until Qc = Kc.
    • Qc > Kc → net backward reaction until Qc = Kc.
    • Qc = Kc → system is at equilibrium (no net change).
  • Key concept: Kc depends only on temperature. Adding acid/alkali does NOT change Kc — it only changes Qc, causing the position of equilibrium to shift.
  • Common exam mistake: Do not confuse "equilibrium shifts" with "Kc changes". Le Chatelier's shifts are about concentration/pressure/volume disturbances; only temperature change alters the value of Kc.

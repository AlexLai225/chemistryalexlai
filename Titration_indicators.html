HKDSE Tips — Choosing the Right Indicator

An acid-base indicator is suitable only if its pH transition range falls entirely within the sharp vertical portion of the titration curve (the "pH jump" near the equivalence point).

Titration Type pH at Equivalence pH Jump Range Suitable Indicator
Strong acid + Strong alkali
(e.g. HCl + NaOH)		 7 ~ 3 to 11 Either methyl orange or phenolphthalein
Weak acid + Strong alkali
(e.g. CH₃COOH + NaOH)		 ~ 8.7 ~ 7 to 11 Phenolphthalein only (methyl orange changes colour too early, within the buffer region)
Strong acid + Weak alkali
(e.g. HCl + NH₃)		 ~ 5.3 ~ 3 to 7 Methyl orange only (phenolphthalein changes colour too early — it decolourises while the solution is still alkaline, well before the equivalence point)
Weak acid + Weak alkali
(e.g. CH₃COOH + NH₃)		 ~ 7 No sharp jump No suitable indicator — use a pH meter instead
  • Methyl orange transitions from red (pH < 3.1) to yellow (pH > 4.4), passing through orange around pH 3.7.
  • Phenolphthalein transitions from colourless (pH < 8.3) to pink (pH > 10.0).

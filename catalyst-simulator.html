HKDSE Concept💡 Key Points

① Definition of catalyst: A catalyst is a substance that changes (usually increases) the rate of a reaction but itself remains chemically unchanged at the end of the reaction. The same final volume of O₂ is obtained in every run with the same volume and concentration of H₂O₂ — only the time required differs.

② No catalyst (control): H₂O₂(aq) decomposes very slowly at room temperature — the curve is almost flat.

③ MnO₂(s) — inorganic catalyst: Provides an alternative reaction pathway with lower activation energy. Higher temperature → more particles with E ≥ Ea → even faster rate. MnO₂ functions well even at 60 °C.

④ Catalase — biological catalyst (enzyme): Highly effective near body temperature (≈ 37 °C, its optimum temperature). Below this, rate is reduced; above ≈ 50 °C the catalase is denatured → curve becomes nearly flat at 60 °C, similar to the no-catalyst case at the same temperature. This contrast (MnO₂ vs catalase at 60 °C) illustrates a key difference between inorganic and biological catalysts.

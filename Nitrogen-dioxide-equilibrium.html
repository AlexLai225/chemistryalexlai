📚 Key Concepts (HKDSE)

Le Chatelier's Principle: If a system at equilibrium is disturbed, it shifts in the direction that opposes the disturbance.

For 2 NO₂(g) ⇌ N₂O₄(g): the LHS has 2 mol of gas while the RHS has only 1 mol. Therefore the position of equilibrium responds to volume/pressure changes:

  • Compression (smaller V, higher P): Both [NO₂] and [N₂O₄] rise instantly. Q = [N₂O₄]/[NO₂]² becomes < Kc, so the forward reaction is favoured. Equilibrium shifts toward N₂O₄ — gas appears lighter brown.
  • Expansion (larger V, lower P): Both concentrations halve instantly. Q becomes > Kc, so the backward reaction is favoured. Equilibrium shifts toward NO₂ — gas appears darker brown.

Note on the curves: A vertical jump on the graph corresponds to the instant of volume change, where concentrations change suddenly (no reaction has occurred yet). The smooth curve afterwards is the gradual re-establishment of equilibrium.
